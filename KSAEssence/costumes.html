Cultural Costumes

1. BISHT

The "bisht" is a traditional Arab men's cloak, tailored meticulously from spun wool and cotton threads. Soft bishts require delicate handling to prevent tearing, while rougher ones offer durability. Al-Ahsa in eastern Saudi Arabia is famous for crafting premium bishts, favored by royalty and senior figures for their bespoke attire.

2. THE MAGSAB AGAL

The "agal" is a symbol cherished by every Saudi, adding a unique charm and distinctiveness to their appearance that they proudly boast. Every detail of the Saudi agal sets it apart from those worn in other Gulf countries. Originally, the Saudi agal wasn't black but had a royal touch, with threads of gold (known as the "magsab" agal). It was a royal symbol worn by King Abdulaziz and his successors, King Saud and King Faisal.

3. ASIRI ATTIRE

The Asiri attire is known for its colorful decorations, diverse shapes, and intricate embroidery, symbolizing the rich heritage of the Aseer region in Saudi Arabia. Passed down through generations, it represents elegance and authenticity. Women proudly wear it on national occasions.
