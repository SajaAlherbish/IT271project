Traditional Food

1. KABSA

Kabsa is Saudi Arabia's favorite dish, combining Basmati rice, meat, vegetables, and a mix of spices like black pepper, cardamom, and saffron. It can include lamb, beef, chicken, or seafood. While a pre-made spice mix is an option, traditional Kabsa offers a unique flavor. Cooking it requires skill and quality ingredients, served hot with sauces or pickles.

2. JARISH

Jareesh is famous in the Najd region. It is made from cracked wheat cooked with meat broth, black pepper, cumin, and onions. It's simmered until the wheat is cooked and the broth thickens. Jareesh is served with Arabic bread and garnished with fried onions and fresh mint. It's also commonly cooked with yogurt. Saudi Jareesh is a delicious and nutritious dish, popular among people in Saudi Arabia.

3. KLIJA

Kleija is a popular dessert from Qassim and Hail regions in Saudi Arabia, made with wheat dough stuffed with date syrup, flavored with cardamom and saffron. It can also use toffee instead of date syrup. It's both delicious and healthy, rich in fiber and carbs, with antioxidants. Often served as a dessert or during celebrations.
